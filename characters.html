Sprocket
Mayor · Mascot · Mystery
Sprocket
Clockwork Griffin — Mayor of the Brass Foundry
Mascot Elected Official Clockwork Griffin Beloved Chaos

Nobody is entirely sure how Sprocket ended up on the ballot. The prevailing theory is that no one was brave enough to take their name off it.

Part griffin, part clockwork marvel, entirely unpredictable — Sprocket is the Brass Foundry's beloved mascot and, against all reasonable expectations, its sitting Mayor. Their feathers are laced with copper filament that catches the light of the Cogspire's chimes. Their talons leave small gear-shaped impressions in soft ground. Nobody has ever seen them sleep, though a maintenance engineer once reported hearing gentle ticking from the mayoral office at 3am.

Their single defining act of governance — opening the Skyrail Docks to the lower districts — was so swift, so decisive, and so wildly popular that political rivals are still trying to figure out what happened. Sprocket has not held a press conference since. They communicate primarily through meaningful looks, occasional memos written in surprisingly elegant cursive, and showing up exactly where they're needed at exactly the right moment.

Whether Sprocket is a political genius, a force of nature wearing a mayoral sash, or something the Foundry dreamed up and set loose — the city loves them, and the city's love, once given, is not easily revoked.

"Sprocket showed up to the opening of the lower Skyrail platform, said nothing, spread their wings once, and every Smogkeeper in the Docks lit their lantern at the same time. I don't know how they did it. I don't think they do either."

— A Boiler Yards worker, on the Night the Sky Opened
Volara
Duchess · Founder · Flame
Volara
Duchess of Brass and Velvet Desires — Feral Mistress of the Brass Foundry
Founder Duchess Guild Leader Defier of Rules Patron Saint of Chaos

Volara was, once, a duchess. She still has the title. She has simply repurposed what it means.

Defier of rules. Breaker of hearts. Patron saint of chaos. Feral Elegance Incarnate. Crafter of Dark Velvet Desires. Spinner of Seductive Chaos. The descriptions accumulate around Volara the way smoke accumulates around the Foundry — inevitable, atmospheric, and impossible to fully clear. She does not correct them. She considers them a reasonable summary.

She was one of the original Soot Circle — though she would tell you the Circle was her idea and she merely allowed the others to participate. Her airship is the finest vessel in the Skyrail Docks and docked in a berth that appears on no manifest. Her quarters in the Cogspire rotate on a schedule only she controls. The other founding members have, over the years, faded into legend. Volara has not faded. Volara has intensified.

She runs her guild from the heights — literally and philosophically. Those who earn a place on the Airship find themselves in the company of someone who demands excellence, rewards chaos, and has absolutely no patience for anyone who leaves their morality on her doorstep and then asks for it back.

"Leave your morality at my doorstep."

— Volara, on the philosophy of the Airship
Murk
Crocodile · Coffee Devotee · Resident Grouch
Murk
The Crocodile of the Rusted Swamp — Top Hat Aficionado, Coffee Absolutist
Rusted Swamp Crocodile Extremely Caffeinated Do Not Approach Before Coffee

Murk does not have a morning personality. Murk has a post-coffee personality and a before-coffee personality, and only one of them is safe to be around.

A crocodile of the Rusted Swamp, Murk has lived in the fog long enough to become part of it. His top hat — battered, slightly mossy, absolutely non-negotiable — is the tallest thing visible above the morning mist, which is how most Swamp residents know where he is and whether to approach. Hat tilted left: amenable. Hat tilted right: do not. Hat missing entirely: a situation is developing.

He is not unfriendly. He is specifically, precisely, grumpy — which is different. There is warmth underneath it, the way there is warmth underneath the Rusted Swamp's fog: constant, real, and requiring some patience to find. Those who have put in the patience tend to stay. Murk tolerates this. Occasionally, he appreciates it.

His relationship with coffee is not recreational. It is structural. The Rusted Swamp runs in part because Murk runs, and Murk runs on coffee — black, strong, and delivered without commentary about how much of it he has already had today.

"I'm not grumpy. I'm calibrated. You're just catching me before I'm calibrated."

— Murk, every morning, to everyone
