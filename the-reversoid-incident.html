THE REVERSOID INCIDENT

EVENT ACTIVE: 4/1/26 - 4/7/26 — 168 HOURS

Something went wrong in the lab. Something went very, very wrong.

⚙ ✦ ⚙
⚠ Otterworks Internal Memo — Do Not Distribute

At approximately 0300 hours, a member of the Otter Squad — who has requested to remain unnamed and will absolutely not be remaining unnamed for long — loaded an unverified crystal into the Otterworks Communal Invention Device, Model 7.

The crystal was labeled. Unfortunately, it was labeled in a handwriting that, under low workshop lighting, made a Reversoid Crystal look sufficiently like a Happiness Crystal that no further questions were asked.

The device activated at full output. The fog turned the color of bad decisions. A sound was heard throughout the Foundry that engineers are describing as "a gear running backwards through time." Every soul within the city walls was affected.

The effects are temporary. Probably. The Reversoid Crystal's effects have a known duration of 168 hours, after which the fog clears and everyone returns to normal. Until then — the Foundry belongs to its worst selves.

The Otterworks apologizes. Sincerely. From a secure location.

💎 The Reversoid Crystal

First discovered in the lower reaches of the Geargrave — which should have been the first warning — the Reversoid Crystal is a violet-black gemstone that hums at a frequency no instrument has successfully measured. Its surface appears to shift when you're not looking directly at it.

When introduced to a powered mechanism, it does not amplify or enhance. It inverts. Whatever the machine was built to do, the crystal makes it do the opposite. A heater becomes a freezer. A lock becomes a key. A Happiness Device becomes something considerably less happy.

Applied to living beings via ambient crystal discharge — as has now, regrettably, occurred — it flips the self. Not the body. Not the memories. Just the orientation. The instincts. The impulses. The part of a person that decides, moment to moment, who they are.

"The crystal doesn't lie. It doesn't corrupt. It simply shows you what you would have been, if everything had gone differently from the start."

— Recovered from a Geargrave scavenger's journal, author unknown

The known antidote is time. Forty-eight hours. After which the inversion collapses on its own, like a gear that's been forced to run backwards finally stripping its teeth and stopping. Until then, residents of the Foundry are advised to be patient, flexible, and to hide anything they particularly value.

// HOW TO PARTICIPATE

  1. For the duration of the event, play your character as their reversed self — not evil for evil's sake, but genuinely flipped. The opposite of who they are.
  2. Ask yourself: what does my character most fundamentally value? Now remove it. What's left?
  3. The reversal isn't a mask. Your character doesn't know they've changed. To them, this is simply who they are.
  4. Other characters may notice something is wrong. Confronting a reversed character is encouraged. Chaos is the point.
  5. After 168 hours, the crystal's effect ends. Characters snap back. The fog clears. Nobody talks about what happened. Everybody talks about what happened.
Sprocket
Mayor of the Brass Foundry — Reversed

Sprocket was always a mystery — benevolent, present, impossible to pin down. Reversed, the mystery remains. But the benevolence does not.

The memos are still elegant. The content is glacial. The Skyrail Docks have been re-tolled effective immediately. Lower district access is under review. The mayoral office has issued a seven-page document on the importance of order, efficiency, and the elimination of unauthorized creative expression in public spaces.

The clockwork feathers still catch the light. The talons still leave gear-shaped marks. But the eyes — the eyes are different now. Counting something.

Volara
Duchess of Brass — Reversed

Volara — feral, chaos-sovereign, patron saint of beautiful ruin — has become something nobody in the Foundry was prepared for.

Proper.

She arrived to breakfast at the correct time. She used the correct fork. She has opinions about posture, punctuality, and the appropriate volume for indoor voices, and she is sharing them. At length. With everyone. The Airship has been tidied. There is a schedule. There are rules. She is enforcing them with the same intensity she once brought to breaking them, and that intensity has not diminished one degree.

This is, by wide consensus, the most terrifying thing that has ever happened in the Brass Foundry.

⚠ STATUS: DISTURBINGLY PUNCTUAL. AVOID EYE CONTACT.
Murk
Crocodile of the Rusted Swamp — Reversed

Murk was grumpy. Murk was calibrated. Murk was the comforting, predictable weight of a bad mood with warmth underneath it. You always knew where you stood with Murk.

Reversed Murk is aggressively cheerful. He is at the door before you knock. He has already made the coffee. He is delighted to see you. He is so glad you came.

The top hat is straight. The smile is enormous. Neither of these things should be possible and yet here we are. Swamp residents who have known Murk for years are unanimously reporting that the smile does not reach his eyes, and that this is somehow worse than anything the real Murk has ever done.
